Serving Pi

Configuring the Wi-fi

Now we have to get our hands covered in flour. Here's the fun part, or the challenging part. We will start by typing sudo raspi-config and press Enter. You will see the "Raspberry Pi Software Configuration Tool" come up. This is the easiest way to change some settings, so we will start here.

Expand Your Filesystem

We need to have Raspian expand to use all of the SD card, otherwise it only is able to use a fraction of the drive's capacity.

  1. Go down to "Advanced Options" and press Enter.
  2. At the top of the list it should say "Expand Filesystem", select and press Enter
  3. It will give you a screen saying "Root parition has been resized. The filesystem will be enlarged upon the next reboot". Press Enter to go back to the main configuration menu.

Configuring The Pi For Your Locale

We have to start by configuring the locale so that our keyboard works properly, if you go with the defaults, from my experience, you won't get your wi-fi password entered correctly because your keyboard isn't properly configured.

  1. Start by using your arrow key to go down to "Localisation Options" and press Enter
  2. then "Change Locale" and press Enter
  3. next you will see a long list of options, which the Space Bar will select or deselect, deselect the "en_GB.UTF-8 UTF-8" and select "en_US.UTF-8 UTF-8" then press the Tab key to highlight "OK" and press Enter
  4. You will see the Configuring Locales options pop up next and arrow down to the "en_US.UTF-8" and press Enter. After a bit you will be back at the main menu again.
  5. Go back to the "Localisation Options" and press Enter and then select "Change Timezone" and press Enter.
  6. The "Configuring tzdata" configuration pops up and you will want to select "US" and press Enter.
  7. Next it asks you for the time zone you are in, select the appropriate one for your location and press Enter.
  8. Go back to the "Localisation Options" and select "Change Keyboard Layout" and press Enter.
  9. The "Configuring keyboard-configuration" pops up and you will want to select "Generic 101-key PC" (unless you have a more appropriate selection for your keyboard) and press Enter.
  10. Next it asks your for the Keyboard Layout, select "Other" and press Enter.
  11. Select "English (US)" and press Enter, and again "English (US)" and Enter, and "The default for the keyboard layout" and Enter, and "No compose key" and Enter.
  12. One final time, go back to the "Localisation Options" and press Enter. Go down to "Change Wi-fi Coutnry" and press Enter. Scroll down to "US United States" and press Enter, and then Enter again to finish.

Configuring The Wi-fi

The next task is to get online.

  1. Still in the raspi-config tool, select "Network Options" and press Enter. Select Wi-fi and press Enter.
  2. Enter your network's SSID and press Enter.
  3. Enter your network's password and press Enter, hit the Esc key to exit to the command prompt.
  4. To verify you are connected to the internet, at the command prompt, type ping -c4 google.com. You should see a few successful pings with response times.

You did it! Your pi is now online!
